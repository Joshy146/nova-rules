Nova Roleplay

Official Framework, Community Standards & Server Rules

IMPORTANT NOTICE: By accessing Nova Roleplay — including our FiveM game server, Discord server, custom CAD/MDT systems, forums, or any affiliated platform — you automatically enter into a binding agreement to comply fully with the rules and policies outlined below. Failure to do so will result in administrative disciplinary actions up to and including immediate, permanent global removal from all current and future platforms.

1. Eligibility & Acceptance of Terms

We are dedicated to providing a high-quality, mature, and secure environment for serious roleplay enthusiasts. In order to safeguard this ecosystem, all participants must strictly meet the following pre-requisites:

  • Minimum Age Requirement: You must be at least 13 years of age to participate in our Discord community, per Discord’s global Terms of Service (ToS). What this means: If administrative staff discover or receive credible evidence that you are under 13, you will be immediately banned to remain legally compliant. No exceptions or appeals are granted based on maturity.
  • Absolute Agreement to Terms: By downloading assets, browsing this repository, registering an account, or authenticating through the FiveM server launcher, you explicitly confirm that you have read, processed, and accepted these guidelines. What this means: Your entry into the server serves as your digital signature. You agree to hold yourself accountable to every line item listed within this document.
  • The Ignorance Clause: Claiming ignorance of the rules does not absolve you of consequences or lessen the severity of a punishment. What this means: Statements such as "I didn't know that was a rule" or "The rules changed and I didn't see it" are entirely invalid during an administrative ticket. It is your personal responsibility to monitor the changelogs regularly.

2. Management Authority & Enforcement Framework

Nova Roleplay operates under an established hierarchy of volunteer staff and administrators who sacrifice personal time to preserve server integrity. To manage the community effectively, the Management Team holds absolute operational jurisdiction:

  • Discretionary Account Removal: Management reserves the right to terminate access or restrict accounts across any affiliated platform at any given time, for any reason deemed necessary to maintain community health. What this means: While staff strive for transparency, if an individual is deemed excessively toxic, counter-productive, or dangerous to the longevity of the server, management may intervene without waiting for a specific rule violation.
  • Progressive Discipline Policy: Administrative sanctions are tracked via a centralized database and generally follow a scale including Verbal Warnings, Logged Account Infractions, Kick Adjustments, Temporary Bans, and Permanent Systemic Bans. What this means: Minor mistakes are handled leniently, but repeated patterns of rule-breaking escalate rapidly. Staff reserve the total right to bypass steps in the progressive chain if an infraction is deliberately malicious or severe.
  • Context-Driven Evaluation: Decisions are never made in a purely mechanical void; staff heavily evaluate intent, surrounding context, roleplay history, escalation pathways, and structural server impact before processing punishments.

Administrative Decision Finality Protocol:

All staff conclusions are deemed final once reviewed by a member of Management. Public arguments, toxic continuous tagging, or flame wars regarding moderation actions anywhere on Discord or within public in-game voice bands are strictly prohibited. Such actions will lead to immediate escalation to a permanent ban. Appeals must be formally written and submitted through our dedicated, private Support Ticket system.

3. Community Conduct Expectations

Every member of Nova Roleplay must practice standard human decency, emotional maturity, and professional restraint. Out-of-Character (OOC) interactions must be decoupled entirely from In-Character (IC) tension.

  • Targeted Harassment & Intimidation: Engaging in a targeted campaign to isolate, intimidate, abuse, or severely distress another player is completely outlawed. What this means: While your in-game character can have rivalries, enemies, and tense arguments, continuing those insults out-of-character via Discord DMs, toxic OOC chat remarks, or stalking a player from scene to scene across multiple days is categorized as real-world harassment.
  • Hate Speech & Systematic Discrimination: The utilization of derogatory slurs, targeted attacks, or biased rhetoric targeting race, national origin, ethnicity, citizenship status, religious beliefs, sex, gender, sexual orientation, or disability is strictly forbidden. What this means: We maintain a zero-tolerance stance. There is absolutely no roleplay scenario where hate speech is justified or permissible. This results in an immediate permanent ban.
  • Out-Of-Character (OOC) Toxicity: Publicly trashing the server, complaining aggressively in global text chats about roleplay quality, or insulting other players' capabilities is prohibited. What this means: If you are frustrated by a scene, complete the roleplay professionally and then open an administrative staff report ticket. Venting or arguing in local text chat breaks immersion for everyone.
  • Malicious Impersonation: Falsely representing yourself as a member of staff, a specialized Real-World Emergency responder, or attempting to masquerade as another community member for personal gain.

Severe Misconduct Protocol (Immediate Permanent Blacklist):

Engaging in Doxxing (releasing personal info), Swatting (sending emergency services to a real home), Network Attacks (DDOS threats), Target Cyber-stalking, or making real-life Terroristic Threats will yield an immediate, unappealable global hardware blacklist. Nova Roleplay will aggressively report all relevant data, IP logs, and system identifiers to federal and local cyber-crime law enforcement agencies.

4. Privacy & Personal Safety

Player privacy is paramount. Real-world boundaries must never be breached under any circumstances, regardless of how intense an in-character scenario becomes.

  • Doxxing & Personally Identifiable Information (PII): Discovering, sharing, or threatening to expose private data belonging to any community member is illegal and completely banned. What this means: Do not post or look up real names, phone numbers, home addresses, family members, workplaces, social security info, or real-life locations of other players. Even hinting that you know where someone lives is grounds for termination.
  • Social Profile Weaponization: Sharing restricted social media links (private Instagrams, personal FaceBooks, Snapchats, or private LinkedIn accounts) to target or mock a player.
  • Real-Life Imagery (Doxxing via Photos): Uploading, saving, or distributing real-life photographs of any player's face, body, or family members without explicit, written, up-to-date consent.
  • Unconsented Recording in Private Spaces: Capturing audio/video from designated private platforms (such as staff support channels, administrative review rooms, or private community meetings) without explicit verbal consent from all present parties. What this means: You are fully permitted to stream and record standard in-game play or public Discord lounges, but recording administrative disputes or staff consultations secretly is strictly forbidden.

6. Cheating, Exploits & System Integrity

Fair play is the cornerstone of our community. Any mechanical attempt to bypass intended engine limits, script frameworks, or system designs ruins the environment for everyone.

  • Third-Party Execution Software: Utilizing external mod menus, injections, aim-bots, wallhacks, custom crosshair overlays, or file modifications that grant an unfair mechanical advantage. What this means: If it changes your recoil, modifies vehicle handling, lets you see through walls, or teleports your character, it is a cheat. Staff utilize automated anti-cheat diagnostics alongside manual reviews. This results in an unappealable ban.
  • Exploiting Game Mechanics & Glitches: Intentionally abusing structural oversights, clipping bugs, or broken script loops for financial, tactical, or positioning gain. What this means: If an inventory item duplicates due to lag, or a wall allows you to shoot through it invisibly, you must stop doing it and report it immediately. Continuous abuse is classified as a deliberate exploit.
  • Away From Keyboard (AFK) Farming: Scripting, using macro loops, or weighting down keys to bypass the idle kick timer in order to collect passive income, server experience, or unlockable items.
  • Real-Money Trading (RMT): Selling or buying server items, properties, custom cars, or priority queues using real-world fiat currency or crypto assets. All server transactions must process exclusively through the official Nova Roleplay store.

The Mandatory Self-Reporting Directive:

If you stumble across a server bug, duplicate item error, broken financial system, or physical wall glitch, you are strictly obligated to file an instant report through our official developer ticketing queue. Knowing about an exploit and keeping it quiet to benefit your group or faction is treated with the same disciplinary severity as cheating.

8. Critical Roleplay Restrictions

The following rules define the boundary lines of realistic interactive storytelling. Violating these mechanical restrictions results in a direct break of roleplay quality and is punished accordingly.

  • RDM (Random Deathmatch): Killing or inflicting severe physical harm upon another player without extensive, deep, preceding roleplay interaction and clear verbal escalation. What it means: You cannot pull out a weapon and shoot someone simply because they bumped your car, looked at you wrong, or because you "felt like starting a fight." There must be a clear story arc or life-threatening interaction leading up to lethal force. ✘ VIOLATION: You see a player walking down the street, sprint up to them, yell "Hey lose weight!", shoot them in the head, and run away.
    ✓ ALLOWED: Your faction has been in an ongoing dispute over turf for days. You corner a rival dealer, hold him at gunpoint, demand his product, he reaches for his weapon anyway, and you fire.
  • VDM (Vehicle Deathmatch): Using any vehicle (car, motorcycle, truck, helicopter) as a primary battering weapon or missile to ram, crush, or execute other players or occupied police cruisers. What it means: Vehicles must be treated as valuable assets that can kill you in real life. Running down pedestrians or repeatedly slamming your sports car into a cop car at 100 MPH is strictly forbidden. Tactical ramming is only allowed for law enforcement executing authorized PIT maneuvers at appropriate speeds. ✘ VIOLATION: A police officer is standing outside his car conducting a traffic stop. You intentionally veer off the road, hit him at full speed to "save your friend," and speed off.
    ✓ ALLOWED: You are fleeing an active robbery at low speed, an officer runs directly behind your trunk unexpectedly while you are already reversing, and you accidentally clip him.
  • Emergency Services Impersonation: Purchasing law enforcement style vehicles, wearing civilian clothes styled exactly like police uniforms, or pulling over players while pretending to be an active Law Enforcement Officer (LEO) or Emergency Medical Services (EMS) technician. What it means: You must be officially whitelisted and hired into the actual departments (LSPD, BCSO, SAFR) to perform public safety duties or drive official government vehicles. Security guards or impersonators are heavily limited and cannot write tickets or make arrests.
  • Green Zone Violations (Safe Hubs): Initiating criminal activities, grand theft auto, hostage-taking, or active fire-fights inside officially designated safe zones. What it means: Areas like Hospitals, Central Police Stations, Court Houses, and designated spawn points are structured as OOC safe hubs. You cannot camp outside a hospital to kidnap someone who just respawned. Criminal scenes cannot start inside these zones under any condition.
  • Cop Baiting & Scene Jumping: Intentionally executing unrealistic maneuvers, blowing red lights directly in front of stationary cruisers, drifting around police hubs, or crashing into active, ongoing crime scenes that do not involve you. What it means: You must interact realistically. In the real world, criminals try to avoid drawing police attention. Intentionally trying to start a high-speed chase because you are bored ruins operational flow for the server.

9. Foundational Roleplay Mechanics & Definitions

To remain an active player on Nova Roleplay, you must master the fundamental mechanics of modern textual and vocal simulation. The terms below represent core rule definitions that dictate how scenes play out:

  • Metagaming: Taking information that was learned entirely Out-Of-Character (OOC)—such as viewing a streamer's live broadcast, reading a Discord chat channel, checking CAD system logs, or hearing it in a private voice lounge—and utilizing it in-character to give your character an advantage. What it means: Your character only knows what they physically witness, hear, or read within the 3D game space. If you see on Twitch that your friend is being held hostage at Sandy Shores airfield, you cannot drive there unless someone calls your in-game phone and tells you.
  • FailRP: Actions that are completely unrealistic, physically impossible, or fundamentally break from what a real person would or could do in that scenario. What it means: This covers driving a hyper-car up a near-vertical mountain cliff side, flipping your rolled car over with your hands, jumping off a skyscraper and acting fine, or continuing to sprint away at top speed immediately after being hit by a semi-truck.
  • Powergaming: Forcing a roleplay mechanic or narrative roleplay outcome onto another player without giving them any realistic or fair physical ability to counter-act or react to it. It also includes optimizing game mechanics over realistic human limits. What it means: Using the /me command to force an absolute result is powergaming (e.g., typing /me cuts off the officer's head instantly and there is nothing he can do). You must always write your actions as an *attempt* and allow the other side to respond to the simulation fairly.
  • FearRP (Value of Life): The mechanical requirement that your character must show genuine fear and compliance when their life is placed in immediate, overwhelming physical danger. What it means: If an armed criminal approaches you from behind and pulls a shotgun to the back of your head while you have your hands down, you cannot instantly draw a pistol from your pocket and try to shoot him. In that moment, he has tactical superiority, and you must value your life by complying with his demands.
  • NLR (New Life Rule): The absolute structural memory wipe and spacing rule that goes into effect the second your character fully bleeds out, passes away, and respawns at a local medical facility. What it means: When you press the button to respawn after a fatal event, your character loses all mental memory of the previous incident. You forget who shot you, where you died, why you died, and you are barred from returning to that physical area for at least 30 minutes. You cannot seek immediate revenge or rejoin an active gunfight.
  • Combat Logging: Forcefully closing your FiveM application, pulling your network connection, or quitting the game server to escape an active, unfavorable in-character encounter. What it means: If you pull your internet plug or alt-F4 while being chased by police, being searched during a robbery, or right before your character gets sent to jail, it will result in an immediate, automatic system ban. If your game legitimately crashes, you must immediately report to the Discord "Crash Logs" channel and try to rejoin the scene instantly.

11. Priority Cooldown Operational Rules

Nova Roleplay uses an official "Priority Cooldown" management framework. This system ensures that our Emergency services and Law Enforcement officers have time to process paperwork, reset assets, and give passive or civilian roleplayers access to police response availability without back-to-back stressful events.

What Is a Priority Scene?

Any highly volatile, high-resource operational event that pulls a massive percentage of active law enforcement units away from normal patrol. This includes:

  • Major financial institution robberies (Pacific Standard Bank, Fleeca Banks, Jewelry Store Heists).
  • Active, multi-party shootouts between major gang factions or cartels.
  • High-speed vehicular pursuits that require more than 3 active police units, a PIT clearance, or tactical air support tracking.
  • Hostage negotiations or barricaded suspect scenarios requiring specialized tactical response groups.

During an Active Cooldown, You May NOT:

  • Initiate New Armed Felonies: You cannot trigger server scripts for robberies, start convenience store stick-ups, or pull out weapons on groups while the cooldown timer is ticking.
  • Escalate Routine Calls into Pursuits: If you are pulled over for a simple broken tail light or speeding ticket during a cooldown, you cannot stomp on the gas and initiate a major city-wide chase. You must accept the simple traffic ticket.
  • Bypass Mechanics via Loopholes: Intentionally trying to split a crime up to claim it "isn't a priority" is a severe offense. Staff discretion applies instantly to anyone trying to cheat the timer.

Permitted Activities During Cooldowns:

  • Standard day-to-day civilian job functions (deliveries, towing services, mining, commercial business sales).
  • Character development storytelling, legal gatherings, car meets, club events, and political interactions.
  • Minor legal disputes, traffic citations, standard police investigations, and non-violent calls for assistance.
SYSTEM MONITORING: The priority status is constantly tracked via an automated system feed in-game and through our official Discord bot alerts. Claiming you "had your UI turned off" or "didn't see the chat announcement" will be rejected by evaluating staff. Pay attention to server notifications before executing any criminal action plan.
