Nova Roleplay

Community Standards & Server Rules

By accessing Nova Roleplay — including our FiveM server, Discord, or any affiliated platform — you agree to comply with the rules and policies outlined below. Failure to do so may result in disciplinary action, up to and including permanent removal.

1. Eligibility & Acceptance of Terms

We believe in maintaining a safe environment for all participants. To join our platforms, you agree to the following eligibility requirements:

  • You must be at least 13 years old to join our Discord, per Discord TOS. If you are found to be underage, you will be immediately removed.
  • By participating in Nova Roleplay (including browsing this repository or accessing the FiveM server), you confirm you have read, understood, and agreed to these entire Community Standards without reservation.
  • Claiming ignorance of the rules does not exempt you from punishment. It is your responsibility to review this document regularly.

2. Management Authority & Enforcement

Nova Roleplay Staff are trained volunteers tasked with ensuring the server is safe and enjoyable. The Management team reserves the right to:

  • Remove users or restrict access to any affiliated platforms at any time, for any reason, in accordance with server needs.
  • Issue disciplinary actions (including verbal warnings, official warnings, temporary kicks, and permanent bans) with or without prior warning. Disciplinary history is tracked.
  • Apply discipline based on context, intent, severity of the infraction, history, and common sense.
All staff decisions are considered final. Public arguments regarding moderation actions on Discord or in-game are strictly prohibited and will be punished severely. Appeals must follow official channels.

3. Community Conduct Expectations

All members of the Nova Roleplay community are expected to act respectfully, maturely, and professionally. The following behaviors are strictly prohibited on all official platforms:

  • Targeted harassment, bullying, stalking, or any behavior designed to intimidate or distress other users.
  • Discrimination, prejudice, or hateful language of any kind based on race, gender, sexuality, religion, disability, or identity.
  • Excessive toxicity that disrupts the immersion of others beyond reasonable in-character roleplay. OOC (Out of Character) toxicity is met with a low tolerance.
  • Personal attacks, releasing personal information (Doxxing), or intimidation of other users or staff.
  • Impersonation of specific users, staff, real-world law enforcement officers, or public figures with malicious intent.

Severe Misconduct Protocol:

Severe misconduct (Doxxing, Swatting, Network Attacks, Sexual Harassment, Terrorist Threats) will result in immediate, permanent removal from all Nova Roleplay platforms and may be reported to appropriate law enforcement authorities.

4. Privacy & Personal Safety

We take the safety of our members seriously. Respect for private life is mandatory. The following actions are strictly prohibited:

  • Sharing private, confidential, or identifying information about another user (Doxxing) without their explicit consent.
  • Posting real names, home addresses, phone numbers, exact locations, or government IDs of other users.
  • Sharing private social media profiles or restricted communication channels without permission.
  • Posting clear images or videos of someone’s face (Real-Life Photos) without their permission.
  • Recording or streaming from designated private spaces (such as specific Discord channels) without the consent of all present parties.

Even if information is publicly available, using it to harass, target, or intimidate a member of this community is still prohibited.

5. Prohibited Content

Nova Roleplay aims to maintain a realistic and professional environment. The posting or distribution of the following content is banned across all platforms:

  • Explicit sexual content, pornography, or depictions of nudity, including hyper-realistic "NSFW" models.
  • Depictions of sexual violence, exploitation, or non-consensual acts (Zero Tolerance).
  • Depictions or facilitation of child exploitation (Immediately reported to NCMEC/authorities).
  • Content promoting or glorifying terrorism, extremist ideologies, mass-casualty events, or hate groups.
  • Graphic, excessive, or gratuitous real-life gore or extreme violence.
  • Facilitation of illegal activity or encouraging other members to break local laws.
  • Links to malware, viruses, phishing sites, or any malicious software.
  • Spamming, repetitive messaging, or posting designed to disrupt the chat or forum.
  • Unauthorized advertising, self-promotion, or solicitation of external services, servers, or communities without management approval.
  • Posting false or intentionally misleading information with the intent to cause confusion or harm.

6. Cheating, Exploits & Server Integrity

To ensure a fair and balanced roleplay environment, any form of cheating or exploiting will result in immediate disciplinary action, including permanent bans for third-party tools:

  • Utilization of third-party external programs, "mod menus," injected cheats, aimbots, or crosshairs designed to give an unfair advantage.
  • Deliberately abusing bugs, glitches, or intended game mechanics for personal gain or to disrupt the server.
  • AFK (Away From Keyboard) farming to accrue time, money, or stats while not actively playing.
  • Attempting to phish, scam, or steal other players' account information, vehicles, or virtual assets.
  • Sharing an account or selling in-game assets for real-world currency is strictly prohibited.
  • Making fake distress reports or intentionally misinforming Law Enforcement about ongoing incidents.
  • Attempting to tamper with Nova Roleplay infrastructure, databases, or API systems.

Self-Reporting Rule:

If you discover a bug or exploit, you are obligated to report it immediately to the development team via the official ticketing system. Knowingly utilizing an exploit will be punished.

7. General Gameplay Requirements

To participate in our FiveM server, players must meet the following baseline technical and behavioral standards:

  • A working microphone and a functional voice setup are required for all players (limited exceptions for players with specific disabilities may apply, but must be pre-approved).
  • English must be the primary language used in all public, in-character (IC) interactions to ensure common understanding.
  • Discord access is mandatory. Many critical server announcements, departmental operations, and rule updates are communicated exclusively via Discord.
  • Follow the staff chain of command. If you have a dispute, address the moderator first, then their supervisor, before escalating to senior management.
  • Usernames, character names, and Steam names must be realistic and appropriate (avoid offensive, sexually explicit, or troll names).
  • Self-promotion of other FiveM servers, gaming communities, or services is not allowed within the in-game world or chat.

8. Roleplay Restrictions

The following specific actions are strictly forbidden to ensure realistic, high-quality roleplay scenarios:

  • RDM (Random Deathmatch): Committing violent acts, including shooting or assault, against another player without valid, preceding roleplay justification. A preceding "reason" like "I was bored" is not valid.
  • VDM (Vehicle Deathmatch): Utilizing vehicles as a primary weapon to strike or kill other players without extensive roleplay justification. (Incidental, realistic car accidents are acceptable, VDM-as-weapon is not).
  • Depicting sexually explicit acts or disturbing, non-consensual scenarios using character models.
  • Engaging in "Hitman," vigilante, or contract-killing roleplay without prior staff approval for a specific storyline.
  • Impersonating Law Enforcement Officers (LEO) or emergency services (EMS/Fire) without being a whitelisted member of those departments.
  • Interfering with passive departments or essential, non-crime-related job functions (such as deliveries, towing, or bus driving).
  • Conducting raids, extensive robberies, or kidnappings in designated "Green Zones" (safe zones like police stations, hospitals, and designated business hubs).
  • Interrupting active roleplay scenes without being an active, justifiable participant. (Known as "cop baiting" or "scene jumping").
  • Creating priority situations (armed robberies, shootouts) while priority cooldowns are active.
  • Intentional immersion disruption (e.g., unrealistic vehicle handling, nonsensical roleplay).

9. Roleplay Mechanics & Definitions

The following are foundational roleplay concepts that all players must adhere to. Failure to understand these is not an excuse.

  • Metagaming: Utilizing out-of-character (OOC) information gained from streams, Discord, or forums for in-character (IC) gain. This is strictly prohibited. Information must be learned organically in the in-game world.
  • FailRP: Engaging in unrealistic roleplay behavior that would not be plausible in a real-world scenario or contradicts your character's established persona. (e.g., jumping a vehicle 100 feet in the air and driving off, driving a realistic vehicle with broken wheels).
  • FearRP: Players must realisticly fear for their character’s life in a situation where they are clearly disadvantaged, especially when facing lethal force (e.g., being held at gunpoint when you are unarmed). Powergaming is prohibited.
  • NLR (New Life Rule): When your character is "killed" (incapacitated and "respawns" or "bleeds out"), NLR applies. Your character has a "new life" and cannot recall the specific events leading up to their death, nor can they seek immediate revenge on the parties involved.
  • Combat Logging: Logging out or forcefully closing your game during an active, hostile in-character encounter (e.g., being chased by police, being robbed, being shot).
  • OOC (Out of Character) chat (local text/global Discord) should not be used during active in-character situations unless specifically directed by staff to pause for administrative purposes.
  • Third-party VOIP (Voice over IP) systems (like TeamSpeak or Discord Voice) are strictly considered in-character (IC) communication unless explicitly in an OOC channel (e.g., Staff Support). You are responsible for everything said.

10. Zero-Tolerance Roleplay

Engaging in the following roleplay scenarios will result in a permanent, unappealable ban from all Nova Roleplay platforms and reporting to appropriate legal authorities where necessary:

  • Roleplay scenarios depicting sexual assault, rape, or non-consensual sexual acts in any context.
  • Roleplay depicting suicide or severe self-harm.
  • Roleplay involving massive terrorist events, mass-casualty acts, extremist ideological violence, or genocide.

11. Priority Cooldown Rules

Nova Roleplay operates under a "priority cooldown" system to manage large-scale criminal events, ensuring LEO (Law Enforcement) forces are not overwhelmed and passive RP can occur.

What Is a Priority Scene?

  • Armed robberies (banks, jewelry stores, convenience stores).
  • Large-scale shootouts involving multiple participants.
  • High-speed pursuits involving multiple LEO units or aircraft.
  • Hostage situations with active negotiations.
  • Other large-scale criminal activity requiring multiple responding units.

During Cooldown, You May NOT:

  • Start new priority scenes, armed robberies, or shootouts.
  • Escalate traffic stops or low-priority police calls into violent situations or high-speed pursuits.
  • Engage in LEO baiting, excessive provocation, or attempts to draw LE units away from their stations.
  • Attempt to bypass priority cooldowns via loopholes or technicalities.

Allowed During Cooldown:

  • All standard civilian and business-oriented roleplay.
  • Standard traffic stops, minor offenses, and low-level police calls.
  • Passive, investigative roleplay from Law Enforcement.
  • General social interactions and character development.
Staff discretion applies at all times regarding priority management. The server chat or Discord announcements will declare when a priority cooldown is active or lifted. Claiming ignorance of cooldown status is not a valid excuse.
